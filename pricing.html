FAQ

Pricing questions
answered.

Is there an income share agreement (ISA)?
No. MakerSquare uses a flat-fee model. You pay upfront (or on a payment plan) and keep everything you earn after completing the program. No percentage of future income, no deferred tuition, no surprises.
What's the enrollment deposit?
$1,000 deposit locks your seat after admission. The deposit is applied toward your tuition — it's not an additional fee. The remaining balance is due by the enrollment deadline (June 22 for the July cohort).
Are there any costs beyond the $5,999 tuition?
Small API costs during the program — typically $20–50 total for Claude, OpenAI embeddings, and other services you'll use. We give you a detailed breakdown before Day 1 so there are no surprises. All tools and software needed for the curriculum are free-tier or covered by your API spend.
Is tuition tax-deductible?
MakerSquare is a professional training program, not a degree-granting institution. Many alumni deduct the program fee as a business education expense if the program maintains or improves skills required in their current career. We recommend consulting a tax professional — we'll provide documentation to support a deduction claim.
What's the refund policy?
Full refund (minus the $1,000 deposit) up to 14 days after enrollment. 50% refund up to Day 1 of the program. No refund after the program begins. If you have a serious personal emergency mid-program, we'll work with you individually.
Can my employer pay for this?
Yes. We issue invoices to companies. Many builders get their employer to cover the cost as professional development. We can provide a proposal letter describing the program's outcomes in a format useful for corporate approval processes — just email us at hello@makersquare.ai.
Do you offer scholarships or financial aid?
We have a small number of partial scholarships available for each cohort, targeted at underrepresented founders and operators. If cost is a barrier, tell us in your application — we'll let you know what's available.
