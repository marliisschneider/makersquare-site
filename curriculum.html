We start where
courses end.

If you've done our pre-work, you're ready for Day 1. No beginner hand-holding — Week 1 opens with advanced prompting and APIs. By Friday of Week 1, you're already building. Three products shipped in four weeks, ending with a live Demo Day in front of Austin's tech community.

↻ This curriculum evolves with the industry. AI moves fast — we update tools, topics, and techniques before every cohort to reflect what's actually current. The structure and outcomes stay the same; the specifics stay sharp.

Before You Start · Self-Paced
Three things before Day 1
Light prep — most people finish in a few evenings. We just want to know you've tried the tools, have a real problem in mind, and are ready to hit the ground running. Prompting depth, product scoping, and your first real automation all happen in Week 1.
Step 1
Try the Tools
Use ChatGPT and Claude for 2–3 real tasks in your work. Come ready to talk about what impressed you, what frustrated you, and where you hit a wall.
Step 2
Identify Your Use Case
Pick one repetitive task in your work you'd like to automate. Write a short paragraph: what triggers it, what steps it takes, and what "working" looks like. This becomes your Week 1 build.
Step 3
Admissions Call
A 20-minute call with our team. We'll talk through your use case and goals. We're looking for genuine curiosity and a real problem to solve — not technical experience.
Week 1

Advanced Foundations
From User to Builder

You've tried the tools and have a use case in mind. Week 1 handles the rest: prompting depth, APIs, webhooks, and product scoping — all in the first three sessions. By Friday, Product 1 is already in progress.

Week 1
Advanced Prompting, APIs & Product 1 Sprint Begins
Monday
Advanced Prompting & Structured Output
Tools: Claude · ChatGPT · JSON
Why most people get mediocre AI outputs — and exactly how to fix it. System prompts, context setting, few-shot examples, and output formatting. Structured JSON outputs AI produces reliably — not freeform text you parse manually. Model selection: when to use Claude vs. GPT-4o. Live build: instructor builds a production-grade prompt system in 20 minutes. Builders write their first domain-specific system prompt and test it on real inputs.
Wednesday
APIs & Webhooks Without Code
Tools: n8n · Pipedream · Make.com · Hoppscotch
What an API actually is — and why understanding this unlocks everything. REST endpoints, authentication headers, JSON payloads explained without jargon. Webhooks vs. polling: why event-driven beats scheduled. Builders wire their first webhook and trigger an AI call from an external service. By end of day, every builder has received data from one system and pushed it to another — without writing any code.
Friday
Product 1 Sprint — Day 1
Tools: n8n · Make.com · Claude · builder's choice
First 45 minutes: each builder scopes their Product 1 brief with the instructor — what it does, what triggers it, and what the simplest version looks like. Then it's a full build sprint with 1:1 support. No long planning sessions — brief approved, then you build. By end of Friday, every builder has a working skeleton of Product 1 running end-to-end.
✓ End of Week 1: Product 1 skeleton deployed and running — not a brief, a build
→
Week 1 Output: Product 1 skeleton live by Friday. You shipped something on Day 5.
Weeks 2–3

RAG, Agents & Interfaces
Two Products, Shipped

Generic AI is a toy. AI connected to your data, your documents, and a real interface is a product. Weeks 2 and 3 are where your builds stop being demos and start being things people actually use.

Week 2
RAG + Agents — Product 1 Ships
Product 1 Ships
Monday
RAG — Connect AI to Your Data
Tools: Supabase · Pinecone · Make.com · Claude
Why AI without context gives generic answers — and why that makes it useless for real business. Vector embeddings without the math: how AI finds relevant information from hundreds of documents in milliseconds. Building a document ingestion pipeline: upload your files, AI learns from them. Builders connect their own industry documents — contracts, SOPs, client files, internal knowledge — to an AI that actually knows what they know.
Wednesday
AI Agents — Autonomous Multi-Step Workflows
Tools: n8n · Make.com · Claude
The difference between a pipeline and an agent: one executes steps, the other decides what steps to take. Designing agent loops that handle ambiguity. Tools calling tools: agents that read emails, query databases, search the web, and decide what to do next — without being told. Error handling, retry logic, and guardrails for production use. Builders add an agent layer to their Product 1 build.
Friday
Product 1 Ships
Tools: All Week 2 tools
Builders present Product 1: 4 minutes, live demo, end-to-end. The bar is higher than a basic automation — if your product uses RAG or agents, show them working on real data. Written feedback from instructor and peers. Instructor written assessment delivered by Saturday.
✓ Product 1: an AI tool that knows your business — trained on your data, not generic
→
Product 1 examples: Industry Q&A bot trained on your firm's documents · AI that reads your CRM and drafts contextual follow-ups · Agent that monitors incoming leads and responds autonomously · Contract analyzer trained on your agreements
Week 3
Interfaces + Voice — Product 2 Ships
Product 2 Ships
Monday
Building Real Interfaces
Tools: Lovable · v0 · Bolt · Cursor
The gap between "it works" and "it's a product" is a real interface. AI-generated frontends: describe what you need, get a full working UI in minutes. Lovable vs. v0 vs. Bolt — when to use each. Connecting your frontend to your backend automation via webhooks. By end of day, every builder has something to click, not just something running in the background.
Wednesday
Voice & Multimodal AI
Tools: ElevenLabs · Whisper · Claude Vision · Make.com
Voice interfaces: real-time transcription with Whisper, natural text-to-speech with ElevenLabs, building a voice agent that answers questions from your knowledge base. Multimodal: processing images, PDFs, screenshots, and handwritten notes with Claude Vision — pull structured data from any document without templates or parsing rules. Builders add a voice or vision layer to Product 2.
Friday
Product 2 Ships
Tools: All Week 3 tools
Builders present Product 2: 5 minutes each. This one has a real interface — demo it the way you'd demo it to a client. Open a browser, show the UI, walk through a live use case. The bar: a non-technical person should be able to use this without explanation. Cohort Q&A and instructor assessment.
✓ Product 2: a deployed AI tool with a real interface — something you could hand to a client today
→
Product 2 examples: Client-facing chatbot trained on your documents · Voice agent answering customer questions from your knowledge base · Document processor with upload UI and structured AI output · Multimodal intake tool that reads PDFs and populates your CRM
Week 4

Ship & Scale
Capstone + Demo Day

Scope and build your third product — something meaningful to your actual work or business. A full build sprint with instructor support, ending with a live Demo Day presentation to Austin operators, founders, and investors.

Week 4
Capstone Build + Demo Day
Public Demo Day
Monday
Capstone Scoping
Tools: Builder's choice
Scope your third product — the most ambitious thing you can build given what you now know. Push beyond weeks 1–3: combine RAG with a real interface, add voice to an agent, or build something that could actually be a business. Instructor reviews every brief individually: is this achievable in five days, is it worth presenting publicly, and what's the simplest version that still impresses? Builders finalize scope and start building by end of day.
Wednesday
Build Sprint
Tools: All program tools
No lectures. Full build day. Instructor circulates for 1:1 support. Async support channel active all day. By end of Wednesday, every capstone must be deployed and demo-ready. Builders practice their 5-minute Demo Day presentation and get peer feedback.
Friday
DEMO DAY
Public · Austin tech community
5 minutes each. Live audience of Austin operators, investors, and prospective Cohort 2 builders. Every builder presents their capstone with a live demo. Q&A from the audience. Documented outcomes shared publicly after the event.
✓ Capstone: a live, deployed AI product — presented to a real audience
→
Demo Day: Your third deployed product, presented live. A public record of what you built. Your first real audience.
