Full Curriculum

4 weeks. 3 products.
All of them actually shipped.

No coding required. Before the program starts, you'll complete self-paced pre-work that gets you ready for Day 1. Then one week of foundations, two weeks of building real products, and one week of shipping your capstone — ending with a live Demo Day in front of Austin's tech community.

Pre-Work · Self-Paced · Complete Before Day 1
Before the program begins
All builders complete six self-paced modules before their admissions interview. No time limit — most people finish in 2–3 weeks working evenings and weekends. This isn't busywork: your pre-work deliverables are reviewed by the admissions team, and the best projects are shared on Day 1.
Module 1
AI Tools in the Wild
Explore ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity. Use each for a real task in your work. Write one paragraph on what surprised you about each.
Module 2
Prompting Basics
Write 3 prompts for tasks in your domain. Test them, compare outputs, and note what produced better results and why.
Module 3
Map Your Use Case
List 3 repetitive tasks in your work that take time but follow a pattern. Pick the one you most want to automate — that's your Week 2 build.
Module 4
Explore Automation
Create a free Zapier or Make account. Complete your first automation — even a simple one. Screenshot it. Come ready to talk about what you connected.
Module 5
Sketch Your First Product
Write a one-page brief for the tool you want to build in Week 2: what it does, who it's for, what problem it solves, and what "working" looks like.
Module 6
Admissions Prep
Prepare your 5-minute intro for Day 1: who you are, what you do, what you want to build, and why now.
Week 1

Foundations
Think Like a Builder

Shift from AI consumer to AI builder. Understand what these tools can actually do, learn to prompt them for professional-grade results, and map a real problem from your work to build in Week 2.

Week 1
AI Landscape, Prompting, and Your First Workflow
Monday
AI for Non-Technical Professionals
Tools: ChatGPT · Claude · Perplexity
What AI can actually do — and what it can't. The mental model for thinking like a builder: you're the domain expert, AI is your execution layer. Live demo: instructor solves a real business problem using only AI tools in 20 minutes. Builders identify 3 tasks in their own work that follow a repeatable pattern.
Wednesday
Prompting as a Skill
Tools: Claude · ChatGPT · Google Docs
Why most people get mediocre outputs — and how to fix it. System prompts, context setting, few-shot examples, output formatting. Live comparison: vague prompt vs. engineered prompt on the same task. Builders write 3 prompts for their domain, test each with 5 inputs, and score the results.
Friday
Your First Workflow
Tools: Claude · Zapier (free tier) · Google Sheets
Map a real problem from your work: what triggers it, what steps it takes, where AI fits. Builders scope their Week 2 product brief — what it does, what "working" looks like, and what the simplest version is. Peer review of each brief. Instructor sign-off on feasibility.
✓ End of Week 1: product brief complete and signed off — ready to build Monday
→
Week 1 Output: A clear, scoped product brief for your first build. You know exactly what you're making and why.
Weeks 2–3

Build
Two Products, Shipped

Automation fundamentals using no-code platforms. Connecting AI to your documents, data, and existing tools. Two deployed products, each presented to the cohort for real feedback.

Week 2
Automation Fundamentals — Product 1
Product 1 Ships
Monday
Automation Fundamentals
Tools: Zapier · Make.com · Airtable
How automation platforms work: triggers, actions, filters, and multi-step sequences. When to use Zapier vs. Make. Live demo: instructor builds a complete end-to-end automation in 25 minutes. Builders connect their first two tools and run a test automation from their product brief.
Wednesday
Adding AI to Your Automation
Tools: Make.com · Claude · ChatGPT · Airtable
Connecting AI models inside automations: AI writes the email, categorizes the input, summarizes the document. Error handling so broken steps don't silently fail. Builders wire AI into their automation and run 10 live test cases, logging each result.
Friday
Product 1 Ships
Tools: All Week 2 tools
Builders present Product 1 to the cohort: what it does, a live end-to-end demo, what breaks and how you'd fix it. 3 minutes each. Written feedback from instructor and peers. This is your first real deployed automation.
✓ Product 1: a working AI-powered automation solving a real problem in your domain
→
Product 1 examples: Automated content drafting pipeline · Lead intake + AI summary → CRM · Meeting notes → action items → task creation · Client email categorization + response drafts
Week 3
Documents, Data, and Multi-Step Pipelines — Product 2
Product 2 Ships
Monday
AI-Powered Document and Content Workflows
Tools: Make.com · Claude · Notion · Google Drive
Processing documents with AI: summarizing, extracting, classifying, and rewriting at scale. Connecting AI to Google Drive, Notion, or your own file library. Builders build a pipeline that takes a document as input and produces a structured, useful output — without touching it manually.
Wednesday
Multi-Step Pipelines
Tools: Make.com · Claude · Airtable · Google Sheets
Chaining multiple AI steps together. Conditional logic: if the AI classifies this input as X, route it to Y. Building pipelines that handle messy, real-world input without breaking. Builders scope and begin building Product 2 — a more complex workflow than Product 1.
Friday
Product 2 Ships
Tools: All Week 3 tools
Builders present Product 2 to the cohort: a more complex, multi-step workflow. 4 minutes each: what it does, live demo, where it's fragile, and what you'd add next. Cohort feedback session. Instructor written assessment by Saturday.
✓ Product 2: a more complex, multi-step AI workflow — more inputs, more steps, more impact
→
Product 2 examples: AI research pipeline → formatted report · Client proposal generator from intake form · Content repurposing system (one input → 5 formats) · Data extraction + structured summary tool
Week 4

Ship & Scale
Capstone + Demo Day

Scope and build your third product — something meaningful to your actual work or business. A full build sprint with instructor support, ending with a live Demo Day presentation to Austin operators, founders, and investors.

Week 4
Capstone Build + Demo Day
Public Demo Day
Monday
Capstone Scoping
Tools: Builder's choice
Scope your third product — the most meaningful thing you could build given what you now know. Instructor reviews every brief individually: is this achievable in one week, is it something worth presenting, and what's the simplest version that still impresses? Builders finalize scope and start building by end of day.
Wednesday
Build Sprint
Tools: All program tools
No lectures. Full build day. Instructor circulates for 1:1 support. Async support channel active all day. By end of Wednesday, every capstone must be deployed and demo-ready. Builders practice their 5-minute Demo Day presentation and get peer feedback.
Friday
DEMO DAY
Public · Austin tech community
5 minutes each. Live audience of Austin operators, investors, and prospective Cohort 2 builders. Every builder presents their capstone with a live demo. Q&A from the audience. Documented outcomes shared publicly after the event.
✓ Capstone: a live, deployed AI product — presented to a real audience
→
Demo Day: Your third deployed product, presented live. A public record of what you built. Your first real audience.
