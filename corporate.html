FAQ

Questions we
hear often.

Do our people need any technical background?
No. The only requirement is that participants know your business well. We've run this with lawyers, account managers, operations coordinators, recruiters, and finance teams — people who use computers every day but have never written a line of code. Domain knowledge is the asset. We provide the technical layer.
Can you run this at our office?
Yes. On-site delivery is available and is often the right call for teams of 12 or more — it's less disruptive to your operations and keeps people in the context of your actual business. Travel and logistics are included in the engagement cost.
Who owns what gets built?
You do. Everything built during the program — the tool, the code, the documentation — belongs to your company. We retain no rights to it. The only thing we ask is permission to describe the engagement category anonymously (e.g., "worked with a professional services firm") when talking to prospective clients, unless you want us to use your name.
What if we want to run this for multiple teams?
We can run sequential cohorts — one team through the intensive, then the next. Each cohort gets its own scoping and builds its own project. For companies with 50+ people to train, we'll work out a multi-cohort plan and pricing in the initial conversation.
How do you handle our proprietary data?
We work within your security requirements. We can operate entirely on your infrastructure, use only publicly available AI tools with no data retention policies, or follow whatever data handling requirements your legal and IT teams specify. We'll address this explicitly during scoping before any agreement is signed.
